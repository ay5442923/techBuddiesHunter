Water Borne Diseases

Water borne diseases are illnesses caused by pathogenic microorganisms that are transmitted in water. These diseases can be spread through contaminated drinking water, recreational water, or water used for washing and cooking. Common water borne diseases include cholera, dysentery, typhoid fever, and giardiasis.

Prevention

  • Ensure access to clean and safe drinking water.
  • Practice good hygiene, including regular handwashing with soap.
  • Avoid consuming food and beverages from untrusted sources.
  • Treat water through boiling, filtration, or chemical disinfection.
  • Properly dispose of human waste to prevent contamination of water sources.
